Design Mantras

The user should not be exposed to the inner workings of the systems

Follow the principle of separation of concerns.

Design your system to be easy to maintain.

Design your systems to minimise cognative friction.

Do it for your user

If the system can do if for they user, then let it do it. Don't ask your users to do stuff they don't need to do

Establish a clear convention and stick to it

For example create some rules like "always use lower case, underscore separated words or phrases for your column names, table names, relation names, trigger names, etc. And pay really close attention to your spelling!"

Be terse, descriptive and meaningful

Consistency of naming conventions is critical

For example create some rules like "always use lower case, underscore separated words or phrases for your column names, table names, relation names, trigger names, etc. And pay really close attention to your spelling!"

Follow the principle of least surprise.

If you expect something to work one way in one situation, it should do the same in the next situatation.

ALWAYS avoid complexity for complexities sake
