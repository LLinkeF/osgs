Design Mantras

Mantra 1: "The user should not be exposed to the inner workings of the system"

Mantra 2: "Consistency of naming conventions is critical"

For example create some rules like "always use lower case, underscore separated words or phrases for your column names, table names, relation names, trigger names, etc. And pay really close attention to your spelling!"

Mantra 3: "Be terse, descriptive and meaningful"

Establish a clear convention and stick to it

Mantra 4: "If the system can do if for they user, then let it do it"

Don't ask your users to do stuff they don't need to do.

Mantra 5: "Design your systems to minimise cognative friction."

Mantra 6: "Design your system to be easy to maintain."

Mantra 7: "Follow the principle of separation of concerns."

Mantra 8: "Follow the principle of least surprise."

If you expect something to work one way in one situation, it should do the same in the next situatation.
